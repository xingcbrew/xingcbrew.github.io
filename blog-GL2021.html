GL 2021

January 2020

2020 was one hell of a year for a little co-living space like ours. We experienced a 90% cancellation rate in March when the travel ban began. Luckily we had a few people get stranded with us for a while - don't worry, they said this was an awesome place to be stuck during the lockdown :)

All of our guests began staying by the week, which turned into months. Now we've just become one big family. A group of co-living creatures trying to survive the pandemic and make it through a never-ending foosball tournament.

At first it was a little difficult to accept that after a short run (348 days to be exact), we were done being a "home away from home" for travellers around the world. More than half our bunks were empty. No one was coming. One at a time guests were flying home.

The truth is... thanks to the pandemic, Gosling's Landing has never felt more like a home than it does now. You wake up in the morning and someone offers you homemade chai tea. Guests sit down for meals and keep each other in check.

So with the new year, we welcome the new Gosling's Landing. We have finally accepted this to be our new, and better, normal.
